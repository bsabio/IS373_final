Description

Bauhaus design is bold, geometric, and functional. It uses simple shapes, primary colors, and clean sans‑serif typography to make information feel engineered rather than decorated. Every element is purposeful, and compositions feel balanced through geometry rather than ornament.

Historical Background

The Bauhaus style originated from the Bauhaus school in Germany between 1919–1933, led by architects and designers such as Walter Gropius, Paul Klee, and László Moholy‑Nagy. It aimed to unite art and industrial production, rejecting ornamental traditions in favor of mass‑produced, functional design. Bauhaus shaped modern graphic, industrial, and architectural design by promoting clarity, simplicity, and rational structure.

Color & Typography Rules

  • Primary colors: red, blue, yellow
  • Neutral base: black and white
  • Strong geometric shapes (circles, rectangles, lines)
  • Heavy sans‑serif typefaces inspired by Futura
  • Structured grid layouts

Why Our Demo Is Authentic

Our Bauhaus demo uses a geometric layout influenced by poster compositions from the Bauhaus school. The primary color palette matches early industrial printing limitations and Bauhaus visual theory. The bold sans‑serif typography reflects the movement's preference for clarity over ornament. The composition is organized through strict geometry, echoing Bauhaus principles that design should be functional, clean, and reproducible.
