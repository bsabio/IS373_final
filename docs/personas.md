# User Personas - MyWebClass.org

## Quick Reference

### Design Student
**Goal:** Submit a design demo and receive feedback.  
**Pain Points:** Unsure of style rules, wants examples.  
**Solution:** Style gallery with authentic demos and submission workflow.

### Instructor
**Goal:** Review and approve submissions efficiently.  
**Pain Points:** Managing many student submissions.  
**Solution:** CMS-based approval workflow.

### Hobbyist Designer
**Goal:** Learn design history visually.  
**Pain Points:** Abstract explanations.  
**Solution:** Live demos with written explanations.

## Detailed Personas

## Persona 1: Alex Chen - Design Student

### Demographics
- **Age:** 21
- **Occupation:** Graphic Design Student (Junior Year)
- **Location:** Urban area, lives on campus
- **Education:** Bachelor's in Graphic Design
- **Tech Savviness:** High - uses Figma, Adobe Creative Suite daily

### Goals
- Learn about historical design movements for coursework
- Submit class assignments (design style demos) to portfolio
- Get feedback from instructors on design authenticity
- Build portfolio with recognized design styles
- Understand the "why" behind design principles

### Pain Points
- Hard to find authentic examples of historical design styles
- Generic design tutorials don't explain historical context
- Needs instructor approval for submissions
- Struggles to understand what makes a design "authentic" to a style
- Limited time to research design history

### Motivations
- Grade improvement and course completion
- Building a professional portfolio
- Understanding design theory for career
- Recognition from instructors and peers

### Technology Usage
- Desktop computer for design work
- Mobile for browsing and quick research
- Uses GitHub Pages for hosting projects
- Active on Discord for class communication
- Expects fast-loading, mobile-friendly sites

### User Story
> "As a design student, I need to see real, high-fidelity examples of design styles like Bauhaus and Swiss design, so I can understand what makes them authentic and apply those principles to my own work."

## Persona 2: Professor Jordan Martinez - Design Instructor

### Demographics
- **Age:** 38
- **Occupation:** Associate Professor of Visual Design
- **Location:** University campus
- **Education:** MFA in Graphic Design
- **Tech Savviness:** Medium-High - comfortable with CMS, LMS, Discord

### Goals
- Teach design history with real, tangible examples
- Review and approve student submissions efficiently
- Maintain quality standards for public gallery
- Track student progress and submissions
- Provide educational resources that students actually use

### Pain Points
- Time-consuming to review student submissions one-by-one
- Difficult to maintain consistency in feedback
- Needs workflow to approve/reject submissions
- Students submit work that doesn't match style requirements
- Hard to keep educational content organized and accessible

### Motivations
- Student success and learning outcomes
- Maintaining high academic standards
- Efficient workflow for grading and feedback
- Building reputation through quality course materials
- Making design history accessible and engaging

### Technology Usage
- Desktop for grading and admin work
- Tablet for reviewing submissions
- Uses Discord for class announcements
- Expects simple, intuitive CMS interfaces
- Needs email notifications for new submissions

### User Story
> "As an instructor, I need a simple workflow to review student design submissions and approve them for public display, so I can maintain quality while giving students real-world portfolio experience."

- **Age:** 45
- **Occupation:** Design History Researcher & Author
- **Location:** Major metropolitan area
- **Education:** PhD in Design History
- **Tech Savviness:** High - publishes online, maintains blog

- Difficult to find annotated design examples
- Needs credible sources for academic work
- Limited interactive design history resources
- Hard to demonstrate design principles in motion

### Motivations
- Academic accuracy and integrity
- Contributing to design education field
- Building credibility as expert
- Making design history accessible
- Preserving design heritage

### Technology Usage
- Desktop for research and writing
- Multiple monitors for comparison work
- Uses citation managers and archives
- Expects detailed documentation
- Values accessibility and semantic HTML

### User Story
> "As a design researcher, I need historically accurate design examples with proper attribution and explanation, so I can use them as credible references in my academic work."

## Design Implications

### For Alex (Student)
- Clear submission forms with validation
- Mobile-responsive design gallery
- Quick-loading pages
- Visual examples over text
- Status tracking for submissions

### For Jordan (Instructor)
- Simple review dashboard
- Bulk approval/rejection
- Email notifications
- Clear status indicators
- Minimal clicks to take action

### For Casey (Hobbyist)
- Non-academic language
- Visual hierarchy
- Bite-sized content sections
- Inspiring examples
- Clear call-to-actions

### For Sam (Researcher)
- Detailed historical context
- Proper citations and dates
- Semantic HTML for screen readers
- Print-friendly layouts
- Downloadable resources
